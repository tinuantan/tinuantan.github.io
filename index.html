Key Observations from the Data
Analysis
COVID Impact & Recovery
  • Domestic traffic collapsed by ~65% in 2020 — the steepest single-year drop in Indian aviation history, driven by COVID lockdowns and flight suspensions from March–May 2020.
  • 2021 remained subdued with multiple pandemic waves suppressing travel, with most carriers running at below 60% of 2019 capacity through mid-year.
  • Recovery accelerated sharply from Q3 2022, with total domestic passengers surpassing pre-COVID 2019 levels for the first time.
  • India's aviation recovery was notably faster than the global average, with leisure travel leading the comeback ahead of business travel.
Market Leadership
  • IndiGo held 55–60%+ domestic market share consistently across 2020–2025, cementing its position as the dominant carrier by a significant margin.
  • The Tata Group's acquisition of Air India in January 2022 transformed the competitive landscape — Air India's market share climbed steadily post-turnaround.
  • Go First's bankruptcy in May 2023 freed up significant slot capacity at key airports, which IndiGo and Air India quickly absorbed into their networks.
  • Akasa Air, launched in August 2022, grew to carry millions of passengers within its first year, validating strong residual demand for new LCC entrants.
City-Level Trends
  • Delhi and Mumbai jointly handle nearly 40% of all domestic traffic, but their growth has been constrained by airport capacity — both are among the most congested in Asia.
  • Bengaluru and Hyderabad posted the strongest post-COVID growth among metros, driven by the IT sector's return to office and international connectivity demand.
  • Chennai and Kolkata are notable outliers — both had not fully recovered to pre-2019 passenger counts even by 2024, reflecting structural and economic headwinds.
  • Tier-2 cities like Indore, Surat, Varanasi and Goa showed above-average growth thanks to UDAN scheme routes making flying accessible to new traveller segments.
Structural Shifts & Outlook
  • Indian domestic aviation crossed 15 Crore passengers in 2023 for the first time ever, a milestone that was surpassed again in 2024 — signalling a structural step-up in demand.
  • The LCC model now accounts for over 85% of domestic seat capacity, making India one of the most price-competitive aviation markets globally.
  • Metro-to-non-metro routes grew faster than metro-to-metro, with regional connectivity becoming the primary engine of volume growth from 2022 onwards.
  • Massive fleet orders — IndiGo's 500+ A320neo family and Air India's 470-aircraft order — point to industry confidence in sustained double-digit growth through 2030.
